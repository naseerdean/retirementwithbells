Market Shock Scenarios

Define up to 4 market shocks. Each applies a one-off fall to all capital wrappers in the specified year, then resumes normal growth.

ⓘ
How market shocks work
• A shock is a one-off drop applied to Pension, ISA, GIA and Offshore Bond balances in that model year.
• Guaranteed incomes (DB, State Pension, Rental, Trust, Annuity) are not reduced.
• Multiple shocks can be added; if the same year is used twice, the percentages stack.
• After the shock year, normal growth resumes.

Monte Carlo Settings

Runs random return sequences using your growth rate as the mean. Shows probability of plan success across all strategies.

ⓘ
What Monte Carlo shows
• Each simulation draws a single average return for the full horizon (simple mode).
• A run is a success if the portfolio lasts until your target age.
• Higher volatility widens outcomes; a lower success % signals higher sequence risk.

IHT & Estate Planning
ⓘ
How the IHT projection works
• Estate at death = property (net of mortgage) + remaining ISA/GIA/OB + other assets + (pension if toggled on).
• Trust income is assumed consumed and does not add to estate.
• Values are taken from the Blended run balances at the chosen death age.
• RNRB reduces by £1 for every £2 that the estate exceeds £2m (taper).
